# CHAPTER 11: "Bebendo com os amigos"

**Summary:** The trio continues drinking while Nina explains more LDI mechanics. They discover an inconsistency — Kim's official element is fire, but he manifested darkness (black flames). The group realizes they're being followed by 22-24 armed people led by Roxy. Kim and Jack decide to flee to protect their refuge, heading to a park where they split up to fight.

**Key events:**
- Nina explains avatar data — Kim is registered as fire element
- The black flames from his transformation were "darkness" — an anomaly
- Kim can use two basic attacks: a fireball and a custom attack
- Maximum 4 active powers per battle
- Kim and Jack notice they're being followed since school
- 22-24 armed people surround them, led by Roxy
- Jack attempts to negotiate; Roxy refuses — they're paid to attack
- Kim and Jack decide to flee to the park
- Nina initially confused, then understands the strategy
- They split at the park: Kim forward, Jack right, Nina left
- Kim faces Roxy + 9 people; Nina faces 7 predatory men
- Chapter ends with all three fights happening simultaneously

**Characters present:** Kim, Jack, Nina, Roxy, 22-24 armed attackers

**Tone:** Tense, tactical, then explosive. A slow burn of realization followed by coordinated chaos.

---

## BEAT 11.1 — The Avatar Anomaly
**Characters:** Jack, Kim, Nina
**Location:** Abandoned police station
**What happens:** Jack pulls up Kim's official LDI avatar data. Element: Fire. But Kim's black flames in the Thunderbolt fight were categorized as "Darkness" — an element that shouldn't exist in normal play. Kim tries to downplay it. Nina is fascinated and alarmed. Jack shows Kim his basic attacks: a fireball and one custom slot.
**Narrative function:** Establishes the rules so the anomaly is clear. Darkness element is impossible — proof that Sangue Primordial is outside the game's code.

## BEAT 11.2 — The Rules
**Characters:** Nina, Kim
**Location:** Abandoned police station
**What happens:** Nina explains power limits: maximum 4 active powers per battle, but new powers can unlock automatically during fights. She's studied this. She offers to help Kim develop his loadout. Kim is grateful but uncomfortable — he doesn't like being studied.
**Narrative function:** Game rules exposition. Nina transitions from observer to coach.

## BEAT 11.3 — The Shadow
**Characters:** Kim, Jack
**Location:** Leaving the station → streets
**What happens:** As they leave, Kim notices something. A flicker of movement behind a corner. Then another. He nudges Jack. Jack sees it too. They've been followed since the school. Nina, distracted by her explanation, hasn't noticed. Kim and Jack exchange a look. Trouble.
**Narrative function:** Tension shift. The slice-of-life drinking scene ends. Combat mode activates in the duo's silent communication.

## BEAT 11.4 — The Ambush
**Characters:** Kim, Jack, Nina, Roxy, 22-24 attackers
**Location:** Street near the abandoned station
**What happens:** Figures emerge from alleys, rooftops, cars. 22-24 people — armed with sticks, knives, chains. Their leader steps forward: Roxy, a woman with brass knuckles tipped with spikes. Kim asks what this is about. Jack tries to de-escalate, offering peace. Roxy refuses: "We're being paid to beat you and take photos."
**Narrative function:** The ambush reveals the scope of the threat. This isn't random — they were hired. Someone with money wants them hurt.

## BEAT 11.5 — The Retreat
**Characters:** Kim, Jack, Nina
**Location:** Streets → Park
**What happens:** Kim makes the call — not forward, not fight. Run. Jack understands immediately. Nina hesitates — she wants to fight. Kim grabs her arm. "Not here. Not on their ground." They sprint. The attackers pursue. The trio weaves through streets until they reach a park — open space, scattered cover, defensible positions.
**Narrative function:** Shows Kim's tactical sense. Fighting 24 people in the open is stupid. Drawing them to favorable ground is smart. Nina learns to trust his instincts.

## BEAT 11.6 — The Splitting
**Characters:** Kim, Jack, Nina
**Location:** The park
**What happens:** At the park, they stop. The attackers are seconds behind. Kim points: "Front for me. Right for you. Left for Nina." No time to argue. Jack nods. Nina, still processing, follows his lead. They split. Three fronts. The attackers arrive and are forced to split too. The chapter ends on a freeze-frame of all three advancing toward their separate groups.
**Narrative function:** The trio splits for the first time in combat. Each will fight their own battle — a test of their individual skills.

## BEAT 11.7 — Kim vs. Roxy
**Characters:** Kim, Roxy + 9 attackers
**Location:** Park — Front
**What happens:** Kim faces Roxy and nine armed people. Roxy leads with brass knuckles. Kim dodges, takes a glancing blow on purpose — gauging her speed. He drops two attackers instantly. Roxy reassesses. This isn't a normal target. Kim grins. The fight is on.
**Narrative function:** Kim's fight shows his combat intelligence — taking hits to gather data.

## BEAT 11.8 — Nina vs. The Predators
**Characters:** Nina, 7 attackers
**Location:** Park — Left flank
**What happens:** Seven men approach Nina with predatory intent. She sees it in their eyes — they're not just paid, they're enjoying this. She smiles coldly. "Vem." She lets them close, then explodes into motion. A kick to the first jaw. An elbow to the second. She's not just defending — she's punishing.
**Narrative function:** Nina's fight shows her technical superiority. These are street thugs; she's trained for this since childhood.

## BEAT 11.9 — Jack vs. The Mob
**Characters:** Jack, 7 attackers
**Location:** Park — Right flank
**What happens:** Jack is pursued by seven people — four women, three men. A punk girl with a knife makes the first move. Jack disarms her casually. He uses their own weapons against them — a chain becomes his tool. But he hesitates when fighting the women, asking them to stop. They don't. His expression darkens. "I tried."
**Narrative function:** Jack's reluctance to fight women is established. When pushed past that boundary, he becomes more dangerous.
