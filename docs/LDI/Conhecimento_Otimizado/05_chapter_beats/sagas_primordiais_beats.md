# BONUS: "Sagas Primordiais I: O Início!"

**Summary:** The origin of the Xakaxi tribe in 1450 A.D. — a technologically advanced indigenous civilization living in harmony. Powa, son of Pajé Yawanari, performs a forbidden ritual sacrificing followers for dark power. Yawanari sacrifices himself to seal the Sangue Primordial, becoming a spirit condemned to watch humanity for centuries. In 1500 A.D., feudal invaders conquer the weakened tribe.

**Key events:**
- Introduction of Xakaxi society in 1450 — advanced tech (carbon fiber, robotic agriculture, Xakaxium metal)
- Key characters: Cacique Xakaxi, Nara (warrior wife), Pajé Yawanari, Kawa (young warrior)
- Powa (Yawanari's son) emerges as a rival faction leader — "Sangue Puro Indígena"
- Powa performs a forbidden ritual, sacrificing followers for power
- Yawanari seals the Sangue Primordial, sacrificing himself
- Tribal technology and memories are erased
- 1500: Feudal invaders arrive, conquer the weakened natives
- Yawanari, as a guardian spirit, watches massacres, wars, plagues across centuries
- 2020s: The modern era — Yawanari discovers social media and loses his mind

**Characters present:** Cacique Xakaxi, Nara, Pajé Yawanari, Kawa, Powa, Tawira (Powa's lieutenant), Feudal invaders

**Tone:** Mythological tragedy meets cosmic horror. The language should feel like an ancient legend colliding with brutal history.

---

## BEAT B.1 — The Golden Age
**Characters:** Cacique Xakaxi, Nara, Kawa, Pajé Yawanari, Tawira
**Location:** Xakaxi tribe — 1450 A.D.
**What happens:** The Xakaxi tribe thrives in harmony with advanced technology — carbon fiber weapons, robotic agriculture, amplifying glasses, Xakaxium metal. Cacique Xakaxi leads with wisdom. Nara, his wife, is a warrior and diplomat. Kawa trains as the heir. Pajé Yawanari oversees the spiritual realm. The tribe is at its apex.
**Narrative function:** Establishes the Xakaxi as a sophisticated civilization, not primitive. Their fall will be tragic, not inevitable.

## BEAT B.2 — The Omens
**Characters:** Cacique Xakaxi, Pajé Yawanari, The tribe
**Location:** Xakaxi village
**What happens:** The sky turns pink. A blood-red eclipse darkens the sun. Animals flee. The earth trembles. Yawanari reads the signs — something is coming. He warns the tribe to prepare. Powa, his son, watches from the shadows, unreadable. The omens signal the beginning of the end.
**Narrative function:** Atmospheric setup. The world itself reacts to the coming catastrophe.

## BEAT B.3 — Powa's Ambition
**Characters:** Powa, Tawira, Followers
**Location:** Hidden ritual ground
**What happens:** Powa, Yawanari's son, has gathered a faction called "Sangue Puro Indígena." He believes the Xakaxi should not share their power — they should conquer. His father's wisdom is weakness. In secret, he performs an ancient, forbidden ritual. He sacrifices his own followers, feeding their life force to a dark power in exchange for strength.
**Narrative function:** Powa's fall is driven by ideology twisted into extremism. His ritual is the source of the Sangue Primordial's dark aspect.

## BEAT B.4 — The Betrayal
**Characters:** Powa, Yawanari, Cacique Xakaxi, Tribe
**Location:** Xakaxi village
**What happens:** Powa's ritual causes a catastrophic surge of dark energy. The Sangue Primordial awakens — raw, unstable, destructive. It consumes Powa and his followers. The village is thrown into chaos. Yawanari realizes the only way to stop it is to seal the power within himself — becoming its eternal guardian.
**Narrative function:** Yawanari's sacrifice is the origin of his spirit form. The power is sealed, but at a cost.

## BEAT B.5 — The Sacrifice
**Characters:** Pajé Yawanari
**Location:** Ritual site
**What happens:** Yawanari performs the sealing ritual. He pulls the Sangue Primordial into his own spirit, locking it away from the physical world. His body dies. His spirit remains — bound to the mortal plane, forced to watch over the seal forever. The power sleeps. But Yawanari is trapped.
**Narrative function:** Yawanari's origin as a guardian spirit. His sacrifice saved his people but condemned him.

## BEAT B.6 — The Erasure
**Characters:** Cacique Xakaxi, Nara, Kawa, Survivors
**Location:** Xakaxi village
**What happens:** The aftermath of the sealing is devastating. To protect the seal, the elders decide to erase the tribe's collective memory — the technology, the rituals, the history. They bury the advanced knowledge, letting the tribe "regress" to simpler ways. The Sangue Primordial is hidden so deeply that even the Xakaxi forget it.
**Narrative function:** Explains why a technologically advanced tribe appears "primitive" to outsiders. The regression was a deliberate protection mechanism.

## BEAT B.7 — The Invasion
**Characters:** Feudal invaders, Xakaxi survivors, Yawanari (spirit)
**Location:** Bravara coast — 1500 A.D.
**What happens:** 50 years later. Feudal invaders arrive on Bravara's shores. The Xakaxi, depleted by Powa's betrayal and stripped of their technology, cannot resist. The invaders conquer, enslave, and kill. Yawanari watches as a helpless spirit — screaming into the void, unheard.
**Narrative function:** Historical trauma. Yawanari's guilt compounds — he survived (as a spirit) while his people suffered.

## BEAT B.8 — The Watcher's Descent
**Characters:** Yawanari (spirit)
**Location:** Earth — 1500 A.D. to 2024
**What happens:** Yawanari is condemned to watch. He witnesses the massacres of 1637, the executions of 1789, the world wars of 1905 and 1939, the towers falling in 2001, the pandemic of 2020. Century after century. Helpless. Alone. The other spirits have long since gone silent. He talks to himself. He makes jokes. It's the only way to stay sane.
**Narrative function:** Yawanari's madness backstory. His modern slang is a coping mechanism developed over centuries of isolation.

## BEAT B.9 — The Breaking Point
**Characters:** Yawanari (spirit)
**Location:** Earth — 2024
**What happens:** Short-form video social media arrives. Yawanari discovers it. Finally — a connection to humanity. But the content is absurd: dances, pranks, shallow entertainment. He watches people waste their potential. Something breaks in him. He starts speaking like them. Dressing like them in his spirit form. "Vovô Descolado" is born — a shaman who has given up on profundity.
**Narrative function:** The tragicomic origin of Yawanari's personality. His humor is not ignorance — it's despair wearing a clown mask.
