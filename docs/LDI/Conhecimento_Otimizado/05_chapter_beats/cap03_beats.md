# CHAPTER 3: "Que tipo de briga é essa?"

**Summary:** Kim enters LDI for the first time, guided by Jack's setup and the AI NeoGuide. He faces Thunderbolt in the arena and is brutally tortured — his leg broken, his face bloodied, his fingers crushed — as Thunderbolt works through a sadistic "checklist." Kim refuses to beg.

**Key events:**
- Jack sets up the SBI (immersor, glasses, tech glove)
- Kim creates his avatar — he looks identical to real life
- NeoGuide assigns him the nickname "Briguento 7856#$76"
- Kim is dropped from a great height into the arena
- Thunderbolt immediately electrocutes him
- Thunderbolt breaks Kim's leg, then his fingers
- Thunderbolt works through a checklist: make Kim kneel, cry, and beg
- Kim kneels when his leg breaks, cries when his face is bloodied, but refuses to beg
- Jack watches from the stands, consumed by guilt
- Kim is on the verge of unconsciousness, still defiant

**Characters present:** Kim, Jack, Thunderbolt, NeoGuide (AI), NexusPhantasm (narrator)

**Tone:** Brutal, visceral, horrifying. The fun action of Chapter 2 gives way to genuine suffering.

---

## BEAT 3.1 — The Setup
**Characters:** Kim, Jack
**Location:** Jack's place
**What happens:** Jack explains the SBI — the immersor, the glasses, the tech glove. He connects everything, nervous energy in his movements. This is his world, and he's bringing Kim into it. He warns Kim that it feels real. The pain is real. Kim, focused on the money, brushes off the warning. The equipment syncs with a hum.
**Narrative function:** Establishes the stakes — pain is real in LDI. Jack's nervousness foreshadows that things will go wrong.

## BEAT 3.2 — The Birth of Briguento
**Characters:** Kim, NeoGuide (AI)
**Location:** LDI void / Character creation
**What happens:** Kim materializes in a white void. NeoGuide, a calm AI voice, guides him through avatar creation. His avatar is identical to his real body — no customization. "Unique identifier assigned: Briguento 7856#$76." Before he can ask what that means, the floor vanishes. He's falling.
**Narrative function:** The ironic nickname sticks. The lack of customization contrasts with the wild avatars other players have. Kim is himself, unadorned, in this new world.

## BEAT 3.3 — The Arena
**Characters:** Kim, NexusPhantasm (narrator), Thunderbolt
**Location:** LDI Arena
**What happens:** Kim crashes onto a blood-red arena floor. NexusPhantasm — a floating stylized skull with biomechanical tentacles — announces the match: "Briguento 7856#$76 — 0 wins, 0 losses. Versus Thunderbolt — 1201 wins, 305 losses." The crowd roars. Kim barely has time to register the disparity before lightning strikes him. He convulses, tasting real electricity.
**Narrative function:** Introduces NexusPhantasm as the LDI narrator. The stat disparity establishes Kim's hopeless position. The immediate attack shows Thunderbolt has no honor.

## BEAT 3.4 — The Torture Begins
**Characters:** Kim, Thunderbolt
**Location:** LDI Arena
**What happens:** Thunderbolt is relentless. He fires lightning bolts as Kim struggles to stand. Kim tries to fight back — a sequence of punches — but Thunderbolt is armored and powerful. Each blow that lands is a small hit. Each blow Kim receives is catastrophic. Thunderbolt grabs Kim's leg and snaps it with a sickening crack. Kim screams. The pain is real.
**Narrative function:** Establishes the power gap. Thunderbolt's sadism becomes clear — this isn't a fight, it's an execution.

## BEAT 3.5 — The Checklist
**Characters:** Kim, Thunderbolt, NeoGuide
**Location:** LDI Arena
**What happens:** Thunderbolt opens a holographic interface with NeoGuide. "Checklist: Make him kneel. Make him cry. Make him beg." He checks "KNEELING" as Kim collapses from the broken leg. He beats Kim's face to a bloody pulp until tears of blood stream down — checks "CRYING." Then he stomps on Kim's fingers, grinding them into the stone. "Now say it. Say you give up. Beg me."
**Narrative function:** Escalates the horror systematically. Thunderbolt's methodical cruelty is more disturbing than rage. Each checked item is a milestone of Kim's degradation.

## BEAT 3.6 — Refusal
**Characters:** Kim, Thunderbolt
**Location:** LDI Arena
**What happens:** Kim looks up through bloodied eyes. His fingers are shattered. His leg is useless. His face is a mask of gore. But he doesn't speak the words. Thunderbolt screams at him, demands surrender. Kim's lips move — Thunderbolt leans in, expecting the plea. Kim spits blood in his face. Thunderbolt rages and attacks again, but the match timer is running. Kim won't break.
**Narrative function:** Kim's core character trait — refusal to submit — is established under maximum pressure. This moment seeds his eventual transformation.

## BEAT 3.7 — The Helpless Witness
**Characters:** Jack
**Location:** Arena stands
**What happens:** Jack watches from the spectator area, fists clenched, heart pounding. He can't interfere. He brought Kim here. This is his fault. Every scream from the arena is a knife in his chest. He should have warned Kim more. He should have refused. He watches his brother being destroyed and can do nothing.
**Narrative function:** Jack's guilt arc begins. This moment haunts him and drives his protectiveness of Kim going forward.

## BEAT 3.8 — The Threshold
**Characters:** Kim
**Location:** LDI Arena (floor)
**What happens:** Kim lies broken on the arena floor, consciousness fading. The noise of the crowd becomes a dull roar. Thunderbolt stands over him, victorious. Kim's vision blurs. But in the darkness at the edge of his vision, something stirs. A warmth. A presence. A voice. The chapter ends with Kim slipping toward unconsciousness — and something else slipping toward him.
**Narrative function:** Transition point. Sets up the vision of Pajé Yawanari in Chapter 4. The "something stirring" is the Sangue Primordial awakening.
