# CHAPTER 10: "O preço do sucesso"

**Summary:** After the training fight, Kim, Jack, and Nina debrief in the real world over drinks. Jack and Nina explain the mechanics of mental energy, weapon modes, and power systems in LDI. Kim reveals he spoke to Yawanari again, causing a tense discussion about prejudice. They end the night with deeper understanding of the game and each other.

**Key events:**
- Kim wakes floating as an observer in the arena after his death
- Jack is dancing around Kim's corpse, planning a PowWow video
- Kim questions Jack's three-stage charged attack
- Nina reveals Kim broke the rules manifesting Xakixi mid-match
- They buy vodka and make "cuba de pobre"
- Jack explains mental energy mechanics
- Nina explains weapon modes (normal and Xamã) and power modes (elementals)
- Kim admits he spoke to Yawanari during the fight
- Nina criticizes Kim's vocabulary; a discussion about prejudice unfolds
- Kim learns that playstyles can be combined

**Characters present:** Kim, Jack, Nina

**Tone:** Philosophical drinking session. Info-dump woven into character interaction.

---

## BEAT 10.1 — Floating Observer
**Characters:** Kim, Nina
**Location:** LDI — Observer mode
**What happens:** Kim's consciousness hovers above the arena. He watches his own corpse below. Jack is dancing around it, recording. Nina floats beside Kim, arms crossed. "First death?" Kim nods. Below, Jack strikes a pose and yells "PowWow exclusive!" Kim groans — even in death, Jack is content.
**Narrative function:** Comedic opener. The floating observer mode is a game mechanic. Jack's PowWow obsession is a running gag.

## BEAT 10.2 — The Argument
**Characters:** Kim, Nina, Jack
**Location:** LDI — Observer mode → real world
**What happens:** Nina confronts Kim about the Xakixi. Players can't manifest weapons mid-match — weapons come from purchased RNG packs. Kim either hacked the game or his power is beyond normal parameters. Kim insists he doesn't know how it happened. Nina doesn't believe him. Jack mediates: "Kim is a lot of things, but he doesn't know how to hack anything."
**Narrative function:** Tension point. Nina's suspicion vs. Kim's ignorance. Jack as peacemaker. This dynamic repeats throughout their relationship.

## BEAT 10.3 — The Cuba de Pobre
**Characters:** Kim, Jack, Nina
**Location:** Abandoned police station
**What happens:** Back in the real world, the three buy vodka, soda, and limes. Jack mixes "cuba de pobre" — cheap drinks in plastic cups. They settle into their spot. The night is warm. The mood shifts from arguing to learning.
**Narrative function:** Ritual scene. The drinking spot is where they process events. Alcohol lowers defenses and opens conversation.

## BEAT 10.4 — Mental Energy
**Characters:** Jack, Kim, Nina
**Location:** Abandoned police station
**What happens:** Jack explains mental energy — the core stat in LDI. It determines power output. Some players have high constant output (steady pressure). Others have explosive output (burst damage). Kim, based on the Thunderbolt fight, seems to have explosive. "You have a very strong mind, Kim. That's why you hit so hard."
**Narrative function:** Game mechanic as character insight. Kim's mental strength is his defining trait. His willpower literally translates to power in LDI.

## BEAT 10.5 — Weapon and Power Systems
**Characters:** Nina, Jack, Kim
**Location:** Abandoned police station
**What happens:** Nina takes over. Hands-free: basic, energy-efficient. Weapon mode: requires RNG packs, weapons have Normal and Xamã forms. Power mode: elemental attacks (primary and secondary elements), three duration types (instant, medium, long). Nina lights up explaining — her favorite system. Kim absorbs it all, asking sharp questions.
**Narrative function:** World-building. Nina's passion for the game mechanics shows a different side of her. Kim's questions show his tactical mind.

## BEAT 10.6 — The Prejudice Argument
**Characters:** Kim, Nina, Jack
**Location:** Abandoned police station
**What happens:** Kim mentions speaking to "o sábio índio doido" again during the fight. Nina calls out his phrasing — "sábio índio doido" is reductive. Kim gets defensive. Jack steps in. The three have a raw conversation about language, respect, and Kim's connection to his own heritage. Kim realizes he's been distancing himself from his ancestry.
**Narrative function:** Character growth moment. Kim's internalized prejudice about his own culture is addressed. Nina's pushback is harsh but fair. Jack mediates but lets Kim sit in the discomfort.

## BEAT 10.7 — Synthesis
**Characters:** Kim, Jack, Nina
**Location:** Abandoned police station
**What happens:** The aggression fades. Jack explains that playstyles can be combined — hands-free, weapon, and power modes aren't exclusive. The best players fluidly switch between them. Kim needs to find his rhythm. Nina agrees to help train him. The chapter ends with the three in companionable silence, drunk, watching the stars through the broken roof.
**Narrative function:** The trio processes conflict and comes out stronger. They're learning how to be a team — arguing, then forgiving.
