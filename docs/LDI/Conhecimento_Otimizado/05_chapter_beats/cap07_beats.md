# CHAPTER 7: "Conhecendo Dona Helena!"

**Summary:** Kim demands answers about his father. Helena has a violent bipolar episode, destroys the house, then breaks down and reveals the truth: she is not his biological mother. She was kidnapped as a child, kept as a sex slave, fled with baby Kim, and prostituted herself from ages 12-18 to keep him alive. Kim embraces her.

**Key events:**
- Kim comes home to a messy house; Helena is dressing to go out
- She asks for money as usual
- Kim refuses unless she tells him about his father
- Helena has a violent outburst, breaking objects
- Kim stays calm, waits it out, takes a shower
- After the storm passes, Helena reveals the truth
- She was kidnapped as a child, sex slave, Kim was placed in her room at age 12
- She fled with him, prostituted herself 12-18 to feed him
- Kim calls her "mãe" and embraces her for the first time
- They clean the house together
- Helena smiles — Kim has never seen her smile
- Kim goes to sleep feeling the barrier between them has broken

**Characters present:** Kim, Helena

**Tone:** Devastating, raw, then cathartic. The emotional climax of the first arc.

---

## BEAT 7.1 — The Same Old Scene
**Characters:** Kim, Helena
**Location:** Living room
**What happens:** Kim arrives home to the familiar chaos — bottles, mess, stale smoke. Helena is getting dressed to go out with friends. She sees him and extends her hand: money. No greeting. No "how was your day." Just the routine transaction of their relationship.
**Narrative function:** Establishes the status quo one last time. This is the dynamic that's about to break.

## BEAT 7.2 — The Demand
**Characters:** Kim, Helena
**Location:** Living room
**What happens:** Kim refuses. He holds the money and says he'll only give it if she tells him about his father. Helena's face hardens. She tells him he'll never know anything about "o merda do seu pai." Kim doesn't back down. The tension escalates rapidly.
**Narrative function:** Kim's first real confrontation with her. He's always deflected with sarcasm. This is different — he's standing his ground.

## BEAT 7.3 — The Storm
**Characters:** Helena
**Location:** Living room
**What happens:** Helena explodes. She breaks bottles against walls, throws objects, screams. The bipolar mania takes over. Kim doesn't fight it. He waits, letting the storm pass. He steps into the shower, letting the water drown out her screams. This is a rhythm he knows — wait it out, then try again.
**Narrative function:** Shows Helena's mental health struggles honestly. Kim's response shows he's learned to survive her episodes. He's not scared — he's patient.

## BEAT 7.4 — The Calm
**Characters:** Helena, Kim
**Location:** Living room
**What happens:** Kim emerges from the shower. The house is destroyed. Helena sits among the wreckage, spent. The manic energy has burned out. Kim sits across from her. No anger. No judgment. Just presence. She starts talking.
**Narrative function:** The calm after the storm. The truth comes not in anger but in exhaustion. Helena's guard is finally down.

## BEAT 7.5 — The Revelation
**Characters:** Helena, Kim
**Location:** Living room
**What happens:** Helena reveals everything. Kidnapped as a child. Years of captivity. At 12, baby Kim was placed in her room — another body to be abused. She couldn't bear it. She fled with him. From 12 to 18, she sold her body to keep him fed. From 18 to 24, she was a mistress to bosses to keep jobs. She's not his mother. She doesn't know who his father is. Her voice breaks. So does Kim's.
**Narrative function:** The central tragedy of the story. Helena is not the villain — she's a survivor. Every cruel thing she's done to Kim was twisted love: she gave everything to save him and now resents him for it.

## BEAT 7.6 — The Embrace
**Characters:** Kim, Helena
**Location:** Living room
**What happens:** Kim moves to her. He kneels beside her. He says it: "Você sempre será minha mãe." The first time he's said it without irony. Helena breaks down completely — years of pain, guilt, love, and rage pouring out. They hold each other. The first hug they've ever shared. Kim sees her smile for the first time.
**Narrative function:** The emotional climax. The relationship is fundamentally changed. The "agreement" (pay back by 18 and leave) is implicitly dissolved. They are mother and son now.

## BEAT 7.7 — Rebuilding
**Characters:** Kim, Helena
**Location:** Living room
**What happens:** Together, they clean up the destruction. Picking up broken glass, sweeping, wiping stains. There's no dialogue — just the sound of work. But the silence is comfortable, not hostile. Helena promises to make less noise when she comes home with friends. Kim smiles. They're trying.
**Narrative function:** The physical act of cleaning is a metaphor for their relationship. They're repairing together, piece by piece.

## BEAT 7.8 — A New Night
**Characters:** Kim
**Location:** His room
**What happens:** Kim lies in bed, staring at the ceiling. Everything has changed. His mother is not his mother. His origins are a mystery. But instead of fear, he feels… lighter. The wall between them is gone. He doesn't know what comes next, but for the first time in years, he feels like he has a real family. He sleeps.
**Narrative function:** Emotional landing. The chapter earns its catharsis. Kim's life is still hard, but now he has something he didn't have this morning: a mother who loves him openly.
