# CHAPTER 1: "Quem é esse cara?"

**Summary:** Kim wakes from a nightmare of being tortured in an arena, faces his alcoholic mother Helena, meets his best friend Jack, and navigates a day of selling candy on buses before arriving at Elite Academy where a new student named Nina arrives.

**Key events:**
- Kim has a vivid nightmare about a man in electric armor in an arena
- Morning confrontation with Helena — she demands money, throws a beer can at him
- Jack waits outside, steals Kim's warm beer, they banter
- Kim sells candy on multiple buses to afford the electricity bill
- They arrive at Elite Academy late; Osvaldo the doorman lets them in
- They change uniforms in the bathroom — a ritual transformation
- Pedro "A Máquina" teaches math in a high-tech classroom
- Nina, a new student with pink hair, enters and sits next to Jack

**Characters present:** Kim, Helena, Jack, Osvaldo, Pedro Máquina, Nina, Elite Academy students

**Tone:** Gritty, warm, contrasting — poverty and opportunity side by side. Dark humor mixed with genuine hardship.

---

## BEAT 1.1 — The Nightmare
**Characters:** Kim (dreaming), Thunderbolt (dream)
**Location:** Kim's dream — a blood-red arena
**What happens:** Kim lies broken in an arena, blood in his mouth, arm bent at an unnatural angle. A figure in electric armor looms over him — cold smile, eyes glowing red through a helmet, a crackling electric staff in hand. The crowd roars. Kim feels helpless rage. Then he wakes with a hangover, the metallic taste still in his mouth.
**Narrative function:** Establishes the LDI world before the protagonist knows it exists. Foreshadows the brutality of Chapter 3. The dream is not just a dream — it's a vision of what's coming.

## BEAT 1.2 — The Waking World
**Characters:** Kim
**Location:** Kim's bedroom / bathroom
**What happens:** Kim stumbles out of bed, brushes his teeth, studies his reflection. Black hair, indigenous features, a bone necklace and leather bracelet. He's 17 but carries himself older. He puts on his characteristic cap, simple band t-shirt, faded jeans, an open social shirt over it. The house is old, squatted, creaking wood floors.
**Narrative function:** Introduces Kim's visual identity and economic reality. His ancestral accessories hint at what's to come.

## BEAT 1.3 — The Living Room Battlefield
**Characters:** Kim, Helena
**Location:** Living room of the squatted house
**What happens:** Kim descends into the living room — a landscape of empty bottles, overflowing ashtrays, stained carpets. Helena is passed out on the sofa, half-sliding to the floor. Kim feels the familiar mix of pity and resentment. He makes coffee, does the dishes, packs two cold beers into his school bag with his books. Helena wakes and the fight begins.
**Narrative function:** Establishes the toxic home environment. Shows Kim's premature adulthood — he is the parent in this relationship.

## BEAT 1.4 — The Morning War
**Characters:** Kim, Helena
**Location:** Living room / kitchen
**What happens:** Helena demands money via DIX, calls Kim a "son of a whore," says she's tired of his judgment. Kim responds with calculated sarcasm, calling her "Your Majesty." Helena throws a beer can at him — he catches it easily and leaves with a parting shot. She yells after him to pay the electricity bill.
**Narrative function:** Shows the verbal abuse Kim endures daily. His sarcasm is a defense mechanism. The beer can catch shows his reflexes (combat foreshadowing).

## BEAT 1.5 — The Friend at the Door
**Characters:** Kim, Jack
**Location:** Outside steps of Kim's house
**What happens:** Jack is waiting on the second step, relaxed, mohawk green dreads catching morning light. Military green jacket, white tee, dark jeans. He steals Kim's warm beer as revenge for the can throw. Kim lights a cigarette. Jack mentions "band rehearsal" to annoy him. They argue about the nonexistent band, then walk to the bus stop.
**Narrative function:** Introduces Jack. Establishes their dynamic — brotherly theft, relentless teasing, deep comfort with each other. The "band" joke is a recurring motif.

## BEAT 1.6 — The Candy Route
**Characters:** Kim, Jack, Bus passengers
**Location:** City buses
**What happens:** Kim sells candy on three or four buses, working the aisle with practiced charm. "Good morning! Handmade candies, one coin each, we accept DIX!" Jack accompanies him, watching with silent admiration. Kim reflects that he really needs a real job. Jack suggests learning to code with a fake ID. They pass the time by recounting the party they crashed on the weekend.
**Narrative function:** Shows Kim's work ethic and survival skills. Establishes their financial reality. The party story plants seeds for Thunderbolt's vendetta.

## BEAT 1.7 — The Gatekeeper
**Characters:** Kim, Jack, Osvaldo
**Location:** Elite Academy entrance
**What happens:** Osvaldo, the gray-haired doorman, greets them with warm familiarity. He's the one who got Kim into this school — and Kim insisted Jack come too. Osvaldo taught Kim guitar for three years after noticing his guitar had only four strings. He gave him strings, then lessons, then a chance at a better life. He waves them in despite their lateness.
**Narrative function:** Reveals Osvaldo's role as Kim's first mentor. Establishes that kindness from strangers shaped Kim's path. Emotional bedrock scene.

## BEAT 1.8 — The Metamorphosis
**Characters:** Kim, Jack
**Location:** School bathroom
**What happens:** In the bathroom, Kim and Jack undergo their ritual transformation. Hoodies and caps come off. Uniforms go on. The rebellious street kids become serious students. They emerge almost unrecognizable — but the glint in their eyes remains. They walk to class.
**Narrative function:** Visual metaphor for dual identity. They exist in two worlds and navigate both. The uniform is armor of a different kind.

## BEAT 1.9 — Temple of Knowledge
**Characters:** Kim, Jack, Pedro "A Máquina," Elite Academy students
**Location:** Classroom
**What happens:** The classroom is a marvel — AR projections, AI assistants, facial recognition cameras, ergonomic chairs, solar-panel windows. Pedro Máquina enters — tall, thin, impeccably dressed in a dark suit. He projects mathematical concepts in 3D holograms. Kim and Jack transform into focused students, working their tablets with surgical precision.
**Narrative function:** Establishes the world's advanced technology. Shows Kim and Jack are serious about their education despite their rough exterior. Pedro's mechanical demeanor contrasts with his surprisingly effective teaching.

## BEAT 1.10 — The Pink-Haired Variable
**Characters:** Nina, Kim, Jack, Pedro Máquina, Students
**Location:** Classroom
**What happens:** The door slides open. Nina enters — pink hair flowing to her waist, sharp eyes, impeccable uniform, designer backpack. She scans the room with calm authority. Pedro validates her enrollment with a wrist-scan. She's assigned the seat next to Jack. She sits with natural elegance. The energy in the room shifts.
**Narrative function:** Introduces Nina. Sets up the love triangle / group formation. Her entrance changes the static equilibrium of Kim and Jack's world.

## BEAT 1.11 — The Aftermath Settles
**Characters:** Kim, Jack
**Location:** Classroom / Hallway
**What happens:** The chapter closes with Kim and Jack processing the events — another day survived. Kim thinks about the electricity bill, the dream that won't fade, the strange feeling that something is about to change. Jack is already scheming about Nina. The bell rings.
**Narrative function:** Chapter timeout. Establishes the status quo before Chapter 2 disrupts it.
