# CHAPTER 2: "A garota nova!!!"

**Summary:** Jack tries and fails spectacularly to flirt with Nina. Thunderbolt arrives with five hired thugs to seek revenge. Kim and Jack lead them to their abandoned annex hideout and dominate them in a fight. Thunderbolt then challenges Kim to a match in Lutas de Ilusão.

**Key events:**
- Jack attempts to charm Nina; she rebukes him coldly
- Five armed men enter the classroom led by Brock, hired by Thunderbolt
- Kim and Jack lead the group to their "headquarters" — an abandoned school annex
- The duo changes clothes, stretches casually, then dismantles the thugs
- Thunderbolt hides behind a pillar, terrified
- Thunderbolt challenges Kim to an LDI match with 1000 coins on the line
- Kim accepts for the money; Jack records the bet as proof
- Kim prepares to enter the virtual world for the first time

**Characters present:** Kim, Jack, Nina, Thunderbolt, Brock, Randy, Tony, Mike, Rock, Elite Academy students

**Tone:** Comedic then intense. Jack's humiliation is pure slapstick. The fight is cool and decisive. The LDI challenge shifts to serious stakes.

---

## BEAT 2.1 — The Failed Conquest
**Characters:** Jack, Nina, Kim
**Location:** Classroom
**What happens:** Jack turns to Nina with practiced swagger, adjusting his hair. "Olá, eu sou Jack, e será o seu prazer me conhecer." Nina doesn't even look up: "Não deu pra perceber com a minha apresentação, ou você tem algum deficit de atenção?" Jack stutters through a recovery attempt. Nina spells her name slowly: "N. i. n. a. E não quero conversar com você. Então VAZA!" The class erupts in stifled laughter. Kim watches in pure delight.
**Narrative function:** Establishes Nina as sharp and impervious to charm. Jack's humiliation is a bonding moment — he takes it well, showing his emotional security.

## BEAT 2.2 — The Doctor's Micro-Smile
**Characters:** Pedro Máquina
**Location:** Classroom
**What happens:** In the aftermath of Nina's verbal takedown, even Pedro "A Máquina" allows the ghost of a smile to crack his stoic mask. It vanishes instantly, but Kim catches it. The professor restores order and returns to equations. Kim and Jack share a look of pure complicity — this moment will be milked for weeks.
**Narrative function:** Humanizes Pedro. Shows the event was universally funny. Cements Kim and Jack's silent communication.

## BEAT 2.3 — Nina Watches
**Characters:** Nina, Kim, Jack
**Location:** Classroom
**What happens:** Throughout the morning, Nina observes Kim and Jack from her periphery. She watches their silent communication — gestures, eye contact, micro-expressions. She feels something unfamiliar: a pang of envy. She's never had that kind of trust with anyone. The sound of the bell ends the period.
**Narrative function:** Shows Nina's interiority. Her aloofness masks loneliness. Establishes her desire to belong, even as she rejects Jack's advances.

## BEAT 2.4 — The Five Men
**Characters:** Brock, Randy, Tony, Mike, Rock, Thunderbolt
**Location:** Classroom doorway
**What happens:** Five intimidating men enter the classroom. The leader (Brock) is tall, commanding. Behind him: a blonde with a ponytail and back tattoos; a broad man with a military beard; a silent young man with dead eyes; a massive brute with a skull tattoo. Thunderbolt — bruised, uniformed, cowering — trails behind them. Brock slams Kim's desk: "Vocês são os desgraçados que agrediram meu amigo na festa de ontem?"
**Narrative function:** Introduces the antagonists. The visual contrast between the five thugs and the students establishes immediate tension. Thunderbolt's cowardice is evident.

## BEAT 2.5 — The Reversal
**Characters:** Kim, Jack, Brock, Thugs
**Location:** Classroom
**What happens:** Kim's hand shoots out like a viper, catches Brock's fist mid-air. In one fluid motion, Kim twists Brock's arm behind his back and smashes his face against the desk. Jack simultaneously trips the first thug who moves to intervene, then presses a pen against his throat: "If I feel a single muscle twitch, you'll get a custom piercing from your chin through the top of your skull." The remaining thugs freeze.
**Narrative function:** Establishes Kim and Jack as legitimate threats, not lucky kids. Their technique is practiced, lethal, and completely calm. Nina watches, stunned.

## BEAT 2.6 — The Invitation
**Characters:** Kim, Jack, Brock, Thunderbolt
**Location:** Classroom
**What happens:** Kim releases Brock, smooths his uniform. "Aqui não é o lugar apropriado. Temos um local especial para lidar com pessoas como vocês." They walk toward the door with deliberate calm. The thugs follow, their fury offset by confusion. Thunderbolt, emboldened by the hired muscle, threatens: "A partir de hoje, vocês vão aprender qual é o seu lugar nesta cidade." The group processes to the annex.
**Narrative function:** The fight moves to a new arena — the annex. Kim's calm control establishes dominance. Thunderbolt's threats are undercut by his obvious fear.

## BEAT 2.7 — What's Your Name?
**Characters:** Kim, Jack, Thunderbolt, Thugs
**Location:** Hallway leading to annex
**What happens:** Kim stops, turns, and asks with terrifying calm: "Qual é mesmo o seu nome?" Thunderbolt stutters: "Thun... Thun... Thun... Thunderbolt." Kim and Jack stare at each other for a beat, then explode into hysterical laughter. "Thun-Thun-Thun-Thunderbolt? Que nome absolutamente ridículo!" They walk into the bathroom to change.
**Narrative function:** Comedic release before the fight. The name is intentionally absurd. Their laughter defuses the thugs' intimidation and shows they genuinely don't fear them.

## BEAT 2.8 — The Pact
**Characters:** Kim, Jack
**Location:** Bathroom
**What happens:** In the bathroom, Kim and Jack change into their fighting clothes. Jack asks: "You remember the last time we lost a fight?" Kim thinks. "We've never lost... since we started fighting together. Except... the Champion." They share a grimace of respect. "Why, you worried?" Jack scoffs. "Hoje é briga fácil! Five guys. Three each. Deal?" They bump fists.
**Narrative function:** Establishes their combat history and introduces "the Champion" — a loose thread. The pact (first to take three wins) raises the stakes of the fight.

## BEAT 2.9 — The Annex
**Characters:** Kim, Jack, Brock, Thugs, Thunderbolt
**Location:** Abandoned school annex
**What happens:** The group enters the annex — a forgotten building with shattered windows, creeping vines, and decades of dust. This is Kim and Jack's sanctuary. They begin stretching casually, warming up muscles. The thugs watch, confused by their lack of fear. Thunderbolt hides behind a pillar.
**Narrative function:** The annex is a character — it represents the space they've carved out for themselves. Their casual stretching is psychological warfare.

## BEAT 2.10 — The Fight
**Characters:** Kim, Jack, Brock, Thugs
**Location:** Annex
**What happens:** Kim opens the fight by stepping on Brock's foot, then unleashes a flurry. Jack delivers a devastating hook. The duo moves in perfect synchronization, covering each other's blind spots. Kim takes three opponents; Jack takes two, including Brock. The fight is short, brutal, and decisive. Thunderbolt trembles behind his pillar throughout.
**Narrative function:** Action set piece. Shows their combat synergy. The short duration emphasizes their skill gap over the hired thugs.

## BEAT 2.11 — The Challenge
**Characters:** Thunderbolt, Kim, Jack
**Location:** Annex
**What happens:** As the thugs lie groaning, Thunderbolt emerges from hiding and issues a digital challenge: a match in Lutas de Ilusão. 1000 coins prize. If Kim loses, Thunderbolt gets immunity from physical retaliation. Kim hesitates — 1000 coins could change everything. Jack records the bet on his phone. Kim accepts.
**Narrative function:** The pivot point. The story expands from street fights to the virtual arena. Kim's poverty drives his decision. Jack's recording is insurance.
