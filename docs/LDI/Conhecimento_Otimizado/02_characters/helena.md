# CHARACTER: Helena
**Aliases:** Dona Helena
**Age:** ~40 | **Gender:** F | **Status:** alive
**Role:** supporting (adoptive mother)

## Physical Description
Helena has mid-length burgundy-wine hair, usually disheveled from heavy drinking. Her face is weathered by years of trauma, addiction, and hard living — deep lines around her eyes and mouth. She dresses carelessly, often in stained clothes. When sober, traces of a once-beautiful woman emerge: good bone structure, expressive eyes. In her redemption phase, she starts dressing better and cooking, showing glimpses of who she could have been.

## Personality Core
- Bitter, aggressive, and verbally abusive — shaped by decades of trauma
- Deeply traumatized — kidnapped as a child, sex slavery, forced prostitution from 12-18
- Alcoholic — uses drinking to numb unprocessed pain
- Hedonistic and irresponsible — parties, wastes money, neglects the home
- Genuinely loves Kim beneath the toxicity — proved by her sacrifice to save him
- Capable of redemption — softens after revealing her past
- Bipolar — explosive rage episodes followed by collapse

## Background Summary
Helena was kidnapped as a young child and kept as a sex slave. At 12, baby Kim was placed in her room by her captors, scheduled to be abused. She fled with him, saving his life. From 12 to 18, she prostituted herself to feed and shelter him. From 18 to 24, she worked as a mistress to keep jobs. By her late 20s, she was broken and turned to alcohol. When Kim turned 12, she forced him to work and "pay her back" — a twisted attempt to prepare him for survival while punishing him for the life she lost. Despite everything, she never abandoned him.

## Current Situation
After Kim forced the truth from her in an explosive confrontation, Helena and Kim had their first honest conversation. She broke down, he reaffirmed she's his mother, and they shared their first hug. She's begun to change — cooking, cleaning, calling Kim "meu bebê," showing affection for the first time in years. Her recovery is fragile but real, and she's trying to be the mother Kim deserved.

## Abilities / Skills
- Survived extreme trauma and built a life from nothing
- Manipulative and sharp-tongued when defensive
- Surprisingly strong will — kept Kim alive against impossible odds
- (In redemption) Learning domestic care and emotional availability

## Key Relationships
Helena ↔ Kim — adoptive mother/son (toxic → redemptive)
Helena ↔ Jack — disapproving but familiar

## Narrative Arc
Cap 1: Introduced as an alcoholic mess — screaming at Kim, throwing cans, demanding money.
Cap 2-6: Off-screen antagonist in Kim's life — the reason he works, his burden.
Cap 7: Explosive confrontation with Kim — reveals her traumatic past and that she's not his biological mother. They reconcile in an emotional embrace.
Cap 8: Transformed — cooking, cleaning, affectionate. Shocks Jack.
Cap 9-12: Continues improving — becomes a source of stability for Kim.
Cap 13: Watches "La Casa do Dinheiro" with Kim, makes him a meal for the first time in 5 years — a quiet, healing moment.
Cap 14: (Implied) Continues her redemption, supporting Kim from home.

## Voice & Dialogue Style
Helena's voice is harsh, bitter, and laced with profanity — a defense mechanism honed over decades. She uses cruel nicknames ("pivete", "seu bosta") and cutting sarcasm. When her guard drops, her voice becomes fragile and childlike, revealing the wounded girl still inside. Her words in chapter 7 ("Aos 12 anos, colocaram Kim no meu quarto") shift from aggressive to broken. In redemption, she uses tender terms ("meu bebê") that feel foreign but genuine.
