# CHARACTER: Powa
**Aliases:** —
**Age:** ~30-40 (at time of death / ascension) | **Gender:** M | **Status:** unknown (likely spirit/dark entity)
**Role:** historical antagonist (Xakaxi tribe)

## Physical Description
Powa is a Xakaxi warrior with a commanding, fanatical presence. He wears modified tribal attire — traditional elements twisted into something darker, more menacing. His eyes burn with zealotry, and his body bears ritual scarification from the forbidden ceremony he performed. Even among his own people, he radiates danger.

## Personality Core
- Fanatical believer in "Puro Sangue Indígena" (Pure Indigenous Blood)
- Consumed by a twisted vision of racial and spiritual purity
- Reckless ambition — performed a forbidden ritual that sacrificed his own followers
- Patricidal in effect — his actions forced his father (Pajé Yawanari) to sacrifice himself
- Charismatic enough to lead a faction despite the taboo nature of his cause
- Motivated by some form of vengeance (against whom or what is not fully clear)
- Paved the way for David Kronos by nearly unleashing the ancient evil

## Background Summary
Powa is the son of Pajé Yawanari, born during the Xakaxi tribe's golden age around 1450 AD. He became the leader of a faction called "Sangue Puro Indígena" — a radical group that sought power through forbidden means. He performed a prohibited ritual that sacrificed his followers for dark power, nearly unleashing the ancient evil (David Kronos) upon the world. To stop him, his own father Pajé Yawanari sacrificed himself to seal the Sangue Primordial, containing both the power and the threat. Powa's actions fractured the tribe and led to its decline, leaving the Xakaxi vulnerable to the feudal invaders who arrived in 1500 AD.

## Current Situation
Powa's current status is unknown — likely dead or ascended into a dark spiritual form. His legacy is the forbidden ritual that forced Yawanari's sacrifice and set the stage for the Sangue Primordial conflict. David Kronos's eventual pursuit of the Corpo Primordial can be traced back to Powa's original transgression.

## Abilities / Skills
- Xakaxi warrior training — elite combat skills
- Forbidden ritual magic — sacrificed followers for power
- Charismatic leadership of extremist faction
- Connection to the ancient evil David Kronos
- Knowledge of the Sangue Primordial's true nature

## Key Relationships
Powa ↔ Pajé Yawanari — father / son (betrayal — his actions forced Yawanari's sacrifice)
Powa ↔ Tawira — leader / right hand
Powa ↔ David Kronos — (likely) inadvertently served his cause
Powa ↔ Xakaxi tribe — traitor / faction leader

# CHARACTER: Tawira
**Aliases:** —
**Age:** ~25-35 (at time of Xakaxi era) | **Gender:** M | **Status:** unknown
**Role:** warrior / Powa's enforcer

## Physical Description
Tawira is a massive Xakaxi warrior — broad-shouldered, heavily muscled, built for destruction. He wields a Xakaxi war hammer with one hand, a weapon most men could barely lift. His face is hard, expressionless — the perfect enforcer. He wears minimal tribal armor that shows off his imposing physique and the scars of countless battles.

## Personality Core
- Ferociously loyal to Powa — follows without question
- Silent — rarely speaks, communicates through action
- Utterly dangerous — a living weapon with no apparent moral hesitation
- Physically overwhelming — among the strongest of the Xakaxi warriors
- Single-minded in purpose — once set on a task, nothing stops him

## Background Summary
Tawira was Powa's right hand during the Xakaxi tribal conflict. He enforced Powa's will, carried out the sacrifices, and protected his leader during the forbidden ritual. He wielded an enormous Xakaxi war hammer, a weapon symbolic of raw, unstoppable force. His loyalty was absolute, making him the perfect instrument of Powa's fanatical vision.

## Current Situation
Like Powa, Tawira's current status is unknown — likely dead or lost to history along with the rest of the Xakaxi golden age. He remains a historical figure whose actions contributed to the chaos that Yawanari had to contain.

## Abilities / Skills
- Xakaxi war hammer mastery — devastating strength-based combat
- Elite warrior training — among the tribe's best fighters
- Absolute loyalty — immune to bribery, persuasion, or intimidation
- Intimidation as a weapon — his presence alone crushes morale

## Key Relationships
Tawira ↔ Powa — enforcer / leader (absolute loyalty)
Tawira ↔ Xakaxi tribe — antagonist within his own people

# CHARACTER: Brock
**Aliases:** —
**Age:** ~20-25 | **Gender:** M | **Status:** alive
**Role:** secondary antagonist (hired muscle)

## Physical Description
Brock is a large, imposing man — the leader of a five-man mercenary crew. Broad shoulders, muscular build, confident posture. He dresses in tough streetwear: leather jacket, boots designed for combat. His face shows the hard life of a hired enforcer — not stupid, but used to solving problems with violence.

## Personality Core
- Professional mercenary — takes jobs for money, not ideology
- Overconfident in the beginning — easily underestimated Kim and Jack
- Respects strength — after being humbled, becomes more cautious
- Not entirely malicious — just a thug doing a job
- Competent enough to lead a crew, but outclassed by real fighters
- Has a sense of humor — laughed at Kim's confidence before the fight

## Background Summary
Brock was hired by Thunderbolt to ambush and beat up Kim and Jack at Elite Academy. He brought four other mercenaries (Randy, Tony, Mike, Rock) and confronted the duo in the classroom. He was immediately humiliated when Kim caught his punch, twisted his arm, and smashed his face into a desk — all in a fraction of a second. Jack simultaneously disabled another crew member with a trip. Though they retreated, Thunderbolt escalated to an LDI challenge instead.

## Current Situation
After his humiliation at school, Brock likely received his payment from Thunderbolt and disappeared from the story. The LDI challenge bypassed him entirely. He was a physical threat that Kim and Jack outgrew the moment they stepped into the virtual arena — but he served as an early demonstration of their combat prowess.

## Abilities / Skills
- Street-level combat — effective against normal opponents
- Leadership of a small crew
- Intimidation tactics and physical presence
- (Completely outclassed by Kim and Jack)

## Key Relationships
Brock ↔ Thunderbolt — hired by him
Brock ↔ Kim — defeated by
Brock ↔ Jack — defeated by
Brock ↔ His crew (Randy, Tony, Mike, Rock) — leader

# CHARACTER: Roxy
**Aliases:** —
**Age:** ~17-22 | **Gender:** F | **Status:** alive
**Role:** secondary antagonist (ambusher)

## Physical Description
Roxy is a tough, street-hardened young woman with a punk aesthetic. She wears combat boots, ripped clothing, and accessories that double as weapons. Her hands are always fitted with knuckle dusters tipped with sharp points — her signature weapon. She moves with the coiled energy of someone used to violence.

## Personality Core
- Mercenary — fought Kim, Jack, and Nina because she was paid to
- Brutal in combat — uses weaponized knuckle dusters without hesitation
- Respects strength — recognized Kim's skill even as she lost to him
- Strategic — led a group of 22-24 people to ambush the trio
- Pragmatic — knew when she was beaten, accepted defeat without begging
- Dangerous but not evil — just another product of a hard world

## Background Summary
Roxy was hired (likely by Thunderbolt or someone connected to him) to ambush Kim, Jack, and Nina after school. She assembled a force of 22-24 people armed with sticks, knives, and chains. Jack tried to negotiate, but Roxy refused, revealing they were paid to beat and photograph the trio. The group split up in a park, and Kim faced Roxy directly. She landed hits with her knuckle dusters, but Kim tanked the damage, methodically took down her crew, and finished her with a chokehold until she passed out.

## Current Situation
Roxy was defeated in chapter 12 by Kim. She acknowledged his superiority before losing consciousness. She's unlikely to return as a major antagonist — she was a paid mercenary, not a personal enemy. Her defeat served to establish Kim's growing combat effectiveness and the escalating stakes of his newfound fame.

## Abilities / Skills
- Knuckle duster combat — sharpened points for deep wounds
- Organization and leadership — coordinated a 22-person ambush
- Street-fighting experience — not a martial artist but a brawler
- High pain tolerance — kept fighting despite Kim's overwhelming strength

## Key Relationships
Roxy ↔ Kim — defeated by
Roxy ↔ Jack — attempted to ambush
Roxy ↔ Nina — attempted to ambush
Roxy ↔ Employer (unknown) — paid to attack the trio
