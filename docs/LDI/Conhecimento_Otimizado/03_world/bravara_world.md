# Bravara

## Geography

Bravara is a country in the ISA universe, serving as the equivalent of **colonial Brazil** in this alternate reality. It features:

- **Dense tropical forests** and expansive natural landscapes.
- **Urban centers** with stark contrasts between poverty and wealth.
- **Peripheral neighborhoods** where characters like Kim and Jack live — dangerous areas with degraded housing, drug and alcohol abuse, and limited infrastructure.
- **Parks and abandoned spaces** used as refuges and combat zones by the protagonists (e.g., abandoned police station parking lot, park where they fought Roxy's gang).
- **The Elite Academy**, a futuristic school located in a more affluent area.
- A climate consistent with tropical/equatorial regions.

## History

Bravara's history mirrors colonial Brazil with supernatural and technological divergences:

- **~1450 CE**: The Xakaxi tribe thrives in the region with advanced technology (carbon fiber, robotics, Xakaxium metal). The sealing of the Sangue Primordial occurs.
- **1500 CE**: Feudal invaders arrive in Bravara, initiating colonization.
- **1637**: Conquistadores massacre and enslave the native population.
- **1789**: Revolta de Durkria — a rebellion met with mass public executions.
- **1905**: First global war impacts the region.
- **1939**: Second World War — global conflict with widespread devastation.
- **2001**: International terrorist attacks (World Trade Center) felt globally.
- **2020**: Grande Vazio — a global pandemic that reshapes society.
- **2022**: Yohualticit creates LDI technology, changing entertainment and economy.
- **2024**: Short-video social media platforms arrive, transforming communication.
- **2030 (Present)**: Kim's story begins. Bravara is a nation of deep inequality, technological marvels, and social struggles.

## Society

Bravara in 2030 is a society of extreme contrasts:

**Inequality:**
- Wealthy elites attend prestigious schools like Elite Academy while students from poor backgrounds rely on scholarships.
- The rich live in luxury while characters like Kim sell candy on buses to pay electricity bills.
- Corruption and crime are rampant in peripheral areas.
- A "Do your best" culture prevails among the lower classes — survival is an individual responsibility.

**Social Issues:**
- Alcoholism and substance abuse (exemplified by Helena).
- Child labor and responsibility (Kim working from age 12).
- Gang violence and street fighting.
- Predatory behavior normalized in some circles (Roxy's gang attacking Nina).
- Weak social safety nets — characters must fend for themselves.

**Culture:**
- **Música**: Bands and music culture are central. Kim, Jack, and Nina are musicians (Jack on drums, Nina on bass, Kim as vocalist). Rehearsals are a key social activity.
- **Bebida**: Drinking (vodka, beer, "cuba de pobre" cocktails) is a common social ritual among teenagers.
- **Redes Sociais**: PowWow, a short-video platform, is the dominant social network. Viral fame is a legitimate path to status and money.
- **Educação**: Schools like Elite Academy offer scholarships (50% for Kim, Jack, and Nina) but maintain strict behavioral codes. Fighting outside school can jeopardize scholarships.

**Key Locations:**
- **Elite Academy**: Futuristic school with AR, tablets, and AI assistants.
- **Casa de Kim e Helena**: An invaded, degraded house in a dangerous neighborhood.
- **Estacionamento Abandonado**: The group's private hangout spot.
- **Risca a Faca**: A nightclub in a dangerous area where Kim and Jack work as security.
- **Anexo Abandonado**: The school's abandoned annex — the group's "headquarters."

## Technology in 2030

Bravara in 2030 features a mix of ultra-advanced and everyday technology:

- **LDI Technology**: Full sensory virtual reality by Yohualticit (2022).
- **SBI Devices**: Consumer hardware for accessing LDI.
- **Classroom Technology**: Tablets, augmented reality (AR), virtual assistants in schools.
- **Digital Payments**: DIX system (analogous to Pix) for instant money transfers.
- **PowWow**: Dominant short-video social media platform.
- **Duber**: Ride-sharing service (analogous to Uber).
- **Document Forgery**: Fake IDs available on the black market (used by Kim and Jack to buy alcohol).
- **AI Assistants**: NeoGuide AI assists LDI players with avatar creation and game mechanics.
- **Advanced Weapons**: Soqueiras (brass knuckles with spikes), correntes (chains), modern firearms coexist with makeshift street weapons.

## Culture

The culture of Bravara in 2030 is a fusion of:
- **Indigenous Heritage**: Ancestral traditions (Xakaxi) still echo through characters like Kim, though much knowledge was lost.
- **Colonial Legacy**: Portuguese language, social hierarchies, and economic structures derived from colonization.
- **Globalized Youth Culture**: American and Asian pop culture influences, street fashion mixing 90s revival with futuristic techwear.
- **Street Smart Mentality**: Survival instincts, fighting skills, and cunning are valued traits. Characters like Kim and Jack learned to fight on the streets before formalizing through LDI.
- **Meritocracy Myth**: The elite preach hard work and talent, but systemic barriers keep the poor struggling. Scholarships (like at Elite Academy) are rare lifelines.
