# Lutas de Ilusão (LDI) — Mechanics

## What LDI Is

Lutas de Ilusão (LDI) is a virtual reality battle arena created by the company Yohualticit in 2022. It is accessed through the SBI (Simulador de Batalhas de Ilusão) device. Inside LDI, fighters possess elemental powers, manifest weapons, and feel real pain. The game operates across two dimensions: MDI (Mundo de Ilusões — the virtual dimension) and MDR (Mundo de Realidade — the real world). LDI has its own ranking system called SDR (Sistema Desafios Ranqueamento) and supports multiple monetization channels.

## How SBI Device Works

The SBI (Simulador de Batalhas de Ilusão) is the hardware required to access LDI. It consists of:
- **Immersor**: Full-body immersion unit that connects the user's nervous system to the virtual world.
- **Óculos**: Specialized goggles for visual and sensory immersion.
- **Luva Tecnológica**: A glove that translates hand movements and allows weapon/power interaction.

The SBI creates a full sensory experience where pain, touch, and environmental feedback feel completely real. Limited edition SBIs can cost between 60,000–70,000 units of currency.

## Sensory Realism

One of the defining features of LDI is its **absolute sensory realism**. Injuries sustained in the virtual world — broken bones, burns, cuts — are felt with full intensity by the player. This creates a high-stakes environment where psychological fortitude is as important as combat skill. The game does not numb or filter pain, making battles psychologically brutal. After high-damage fights, players may need up to 96 hours of in-game recovery time before returning to full strength.

## Combat Modes

LDI features three primary combat modes that can be combined during battle:

### Free Hands Mode
The basic fighting style. It consumes the least amount of energy but offers no weapon advantages. Relies purely on the player's martial arts skills and physical prowess. All other styles are inherently more powerful, but Free Hands allows for the most energy-efficient sustained combat.

### Weapon Mode
Players can acquire weapons through randomized loot packages purchased with in-game points. Weapons have two states:
- **Normal Mode**: Standard weapon functionality.
- **Xamã Mode**: An enhanced, more powerful form of the weapon that consumes additional energy.

Some players manifest unique personalized weapons (e.g., Kim's Xakixi — dual axes that combine into a double axe with a steel cable). Weapon manifestation outside the normal acquisition system is considered anomalous.

### Power Mode
Uses elemental affinities (primary or secondary). Powers have three duration types:
- **Instantâneo**: Immediate effect, single-use burst.
- **Médio**: Sustained effect over a moderate duration.
- **Longo**: Extended effect, high energy consumption.

A player can have a maximum of **4 active powers per battle**. New powers can unlock spontaneously during combat.

## Elemental System

### Primary Elements
Players connect to elemental forces upon entering LDI. The six primary elements are:
1. **Fire** (Fogo)
2. **Water** (Água)
3. **Air** (Ar)
4. **Earth** (Terra)
5. **Light** (Luz)
6. **Darkness** (Escuridão)

Each player is officially assigned one primary element upon registration. However, anomalies exist — some players (like Kim) can manifest elements outside their registered affinity, such as manifesting Darkness (chamas negras) while being registered as Fire.

### Elemental Interactions
Elements can combine during combat for advanced techniques. A player's mental strength determines how effectively they can channel elemental power. Energy manifests in two forms:
- **Poder Constante**: Steady, sustained power output.
- **Poder Explosivo**: High-damage bursts with limited duration.

## Weapon System

Weapons in LDI are acquired through:
- **Purchased Packages**: Randomized loot boxes bought with in-game points (moedas).
- **Spontaneous Manifestation**: Rare, unexplained events where a player's soul/essence creates a unique weapon (e.g., Kim's Xakixi).

Each weapon has a Normal Mode and a Xamã Mode. The Xamã Mode is significantly more powerful but drains the player's energy rapidly. Some techniques require specific weapons to execute (e.g., Shuntaro's "Rei Xamã" creates a defensive chainsaw-like construct on his arm).

## Energy System

Energy is the resource that governs all combat actions in LDI. Key mechanics:
- **Free Hands Mode** consumes the least energy.
- **Weapon Mode** and **Power Mode** consume progressively more.
- **Xamã Mode** techniques are extremely energy-intensive and time-limited.
- Players must manage energy carefully; running out leaves them vulnerable.
- Mental strength directly correlates to energy reserves and recovery rate.
- After severe damage, players enter a recovery period (up to 96 hours) during which their energy and combat effectiveness are reduced.

## Death & Recovery

Death in LDI is temporary but psychologically harrowing due to sensory realism:
- In **training rooms**: Dead players must wait for a new session to be created.
- In **ranked battles**: Death results in ranking loss and mandatory recovery time.
- Recovery can be accelerated using the **Sistema de Recuperação** in private training rooms, which heals the player instantly for training purposes.
- The experience of dying in LDI is described as deeply unsettling due to the full sensory feedback.

## Ranking System (SDR)

The SDR (Sistema Desafios Ranqueamento) is LDI's global ranking system:
- Players are ranked worldwide based on wins, losses, and performance.
- High-ranked players gain fame, sponsorship opportunities, and monetary rewards.
- Examples of ranking positions:
  - Kim jumped to position **1,201** after his first battle (with Primordial awakening).
  - Shuntaro Chirasu held position **998** worldwide.
  - Lisa is in the **top 500**.
  - Jack reached **10,776** and Nina **8,724** after betting on Kim.
- The top ranks are watched closely by the community; high-level fighters gain celebrity status.

## Battle Formats

LDI supports multiple battle formats:
- **Public Arenas**: Open matches where spectators can watch and bet.
- **Private Training Rooms**: Closed sessions for practice, with full recovery options.
- **Challenge Matches**: One-on-one duels initiated by player challenge (e.g., Shuntaro traveling from Azuma to challenge Kim).
- **Ranked Battles**: Official matches that affect SDR standings.
- **Spectator Mode**: Eliminated players can watch ongoing battles as floating observers.

## Entry & Exit Rules

**Entry:**
- Players must have an SBI device.
- A NeoGuide AI assists with avatar creation on first login.
- Players receive a unique nickname (e.g., "Briguento 7856#$76").
- New players start at 0 wins, 0 losses.
- Players can maintain anonymity by changing nicknames and avatars, though this is not foolproof.

**Exit:**
- Players can leave battles voluntarily (defeat or forfeit).
- Death in the arena forces automatic exit.
- After severe physical/psychological trauma, the system may forcibly disconnect the player.
- Players can log out between battles freely.
