# CHAPTER 9: "Começando o treinamento?"

**Summary:** The trio enters a private LDI training room. Kim is weakened from his previous injuries and uses the recovery system. Jack and Nina reveal their avatars (steampunk and gothic/punk). Kim manifests his exclusive weapon — the Xakixi (twin hatchets that combine into a double-bladed axe). He has another vision of Yawanari who warns of dangers ahead. Jack defeats Kim with his "Vitória Finals" attack.

**Key events:**
- Kim, Jack, and Nina enter a private LDI training room
- Kim's injuries from the Thunderbolt fight require 96 hours to heal
- They use the Recovery System to heal him instantly
- Jack's avatar: steampunk style
- Nina's avatar: gothic/punk style
- Jack and Nina fight Kim together to test him
- Kim manifests the Xakixi — twin hatchets
- Kim connects the hatchets into a double-bladed axe
- Yawanari appears briefly to warn Kim of dangers
- Kim bisects Nina with the axe cable, eliminating her
- Jack uses "Vitória Finals" — raises stones that fracture into blades
- Kim is defeated by Jack's attack
- Kim experiences death in the game for the first time

**Characters present:** Kim, Jack, Nina, Pajé Yawanari (vision)

**Tone:** Training-montage energy. Fun, experimental, with moments of genuine danger and revelation.

---

## BEAT 9.1 — The Private Room
**Characters:** Kim, Jack, Nina
**Location:** LDI — Private training arena
**What happens:** The trio syncs their SBIs and enters a private training room — a customizable white space that can become any environment. Kim is still reluctant about keeping the expensive SBI. Nina takes charge of the interface, setting parameters. Jack stretches theatrically. They're in.
**Narrative function:** The private room is their training base going forward. Nina's technical knowledge takes center stage.

## BEAT 9.2 — The Recovery
**Characters:** Kim, Jack, Nina
**Location:** LDI training room
**What happens:** Kim checks his status. His body is still damaged from the Thunderbolt fight — 96 hours until natural recovery. Jack explains the Recovery System. Nina activates it. Kim's body glows and knits itself back together. He feels strength return. He's ready.
**Narrative function:** Establishes LDI mechanics. Recovery Systems are a key resource. The 96-hour rule creates stakes for future injuries.

## BEAT 9.3 — Avatar Reveals
**Characters:** Jack, Nina
**Location:** LDI training room
**What happens:** Jack and Nina transform into their avatars. Jack becomes a steampunk fighter — goggles, brass fittings, a mechanical arm, a cane weapon. Nina becomes gothic/punk — dark makeup, leather, spikes, a bass guitar that doubles as a weapon. Kim, unchanged, looks plain in comparison. "You two are extra." They grin.
**Narrative function:** Visualizes their personalities through avatar design. Jack is theatrical and retro. Nina is sharp and aggressive. Kim's simplicity contrasts with their flair.

## BEAT 9.4 — Two on One
**Characters:** Kim, Jack, Nina
**Location:** LDI training room
**What happens:** Jack and Nina decide to fight Kim together. He's the unknown variable — they need to see what he can do. The fight begins. Kim is overwhelmed initially — their coordination is good, and he's still learning the game's physics. But he's a fast learner. He starts reading their patterns.
**Narrative function:** Training fight — low stakes, high learning. Establishes that Kim is outmatched in experience but makes up for it in instinct.

## BEAT 9.5 — The Xakixi Manifest
**Characters:** Kim, Jack, Nina
**Location:** LDI training room
**What happens:** Mid-fight, Kim's hands grasp for something. The game responds. Twin hatchets materialize — Xakixi. The metal is carved with Xakaxi tribal patterns. They feel natural in his hands. Kim throws one, then recalls it by the cable. He connects them into a double-bladed axe. The weapon is part of him.
**Narrative function:** The weapon is an extension of his identity. The Xakixi is tied to his heritage, the Sangue Primordial, and Yawanari. It manifested because he needed it — the game isn't fully in control.

## BEAT 9.6 — Yawanari's Warning
**Characters:** Kim, Pajé Yawanari
**Location:** LDI training room (vision layer)
**What happens:** In the heat of battle, time stops. Yawanari appears beside Kim. He's serious — no jokes. "The power is waking up. There are things coming for you. Old things. I can't protect you in there." He mentions the "Responsa do Sangue Primordial." Kim doesn't understand. Yawanari fades. Time resumes.
**Narrative function:** Raises the stakes. The Sangue Primordial isn't just a power-up — it's a burden with consequences. Yawanari's fear signals that Kim is in real danger.

## BEAT 9.7 — Nina Falls
**Characters:** Kim, Nina
**Location:** LDI training room
**What happens:** Kim channels his confusion into action. He swings the Xakixi axe, cable extending. The blade wraps around Nina and bisects her at the waist. She dissolves into particles — eliminated. In private training, death means waiting for a new session. Nina materializes in spectator mode, shaking her head in grudging respect.
**Narrative function:** Kim's power is real. He defeated an experienced player with a weapon he manifested minutes ago. Nina's elimination establishes a hierarchy: Kim is raw but dangerous.

## BEAT 9.8 — Vitória Finals
**Characters:** Kim, Jack
**Location:** LDI training room
**What happens:** Jack and Kim face off. Jack hasn't held back yet. He raises his cane, "Vitória." The ground rumbles. Stones rise and fracture into thousands of blade-sharp shards. He fires them like a machine gun at Kim. Kim dodges, blocks, takes hits. But it's too much. He's pinned, torn, and finally falls. Jack celebrates — then checks on Kim.
**Narrative function:** Jack's power reveal. He's not just comic relief — he's a legitimate threat. The "Vitória Finals" shows his style is tactical and overwhelming.

## BEAT 9.9 — First Death
**Characters:** Kim
**Location:** LDI — Between states
**What happens:** Kim's consciousness fades. He feels himself die in the game — a strange sensation, cold and empty. The world dissolves. He floats in darkness. Then he respawns, gasping. Jack is laughing. Kim punches his arm. The training session ends. They have a baseline now.
**Narrative function:** Introduces the death/respawn mechanic. Kim's first death is disorienting but not traumatic — it's part of the game. Establishes that in LDI, death is temporary, but the experience still matters.
