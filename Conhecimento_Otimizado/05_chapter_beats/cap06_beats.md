# CHAPTER 6: "Não me julgue"

**Summary:** Kim, Jack, and Nina encounter a kid being beaten by seven bullies. Kim empowers the victim to fight back instead of saving him. After, they buy alcohol with fake IDs and retreat to an abandoned police station, where Jack and Nina explain the full LDI system to Kim. They get drunk, flirt, and solidify their trio bond.

**Key events:**
- Kim, Jack, and Nina witness a kid being bullied by seven students
- Kim philosophically refuses to fight for the kid — forces him to defend himself
- Jack blocks a kick; the victim kicks back, triggering a brawl
- The trio wins the fight quickly
- They buy vodka with fake IDs
- At the abandoned police station, Jack and Nina explain LDI systems (SDR, SDA, SDD, SAL, STE, SDP, SVN)
- Kim discovers he's ranked 1,201st globally — high relevance
- He can remain anonymous by changing his nickname and avatar
- They drink, flirt — Nina provocatively says she'd "take both" but they "aren't man enough"
- Kim arrives home drunk to find Helena in a good mood for once

**Characters present:** Kim, Jack, Nina, Samuel (victim), 7 bullies, Helena

**Tone:** Philosophical, drunken, warm. Mix of street philosophy, info-dumping, and teenage bonding.

---

## BEAT 6.1 — The Beating
**Characters:** Kim, Jack, Nina, Samuel (victim), 7 bullies
**Location:** Street near school
**What happens:** A kid (Samuel) is getting beaten by seven students. Nina moves to intervene. Kim stops her. He walks up to Samuel, ignoring the bullies. "Você quer que eu resolva isso pra você?" Samuel nods desperately. Kim shakes his head. "Então você nunca vai aprender. Se levanta e luta."
**Narrative function:** Kim's philosophy of self-reliance. He won't be a savior — he'll be a catalyst. This is how he treats his own life.

## BEAT 6.2 — The Intervention
**Characters:** Kim, Jack, Nina, Samuel, Bullies
**Location:** Street
**What happens:** Jack blocks a kick aimed at Samuel's head, holding the bully's leg mid-air. "Vai ficar parado? Eu não vou segurar o pé dele o dia todo." Samuel, terrified, finally throws a punch. It connects. The fight ignites. Kim, Jack, and Nina move in sync, neutralizing the seven bullies in moments. They don't do all the work — they tip the scales.
**Narrative function:** The trio's first fight as a unit. Samuel's participation is essential to the lesson.

## BEAT 6.3 — The Liquor Run
**Characters:** Kim, Jack, Nina
**Location:** Market / liquor store
**What happens:** Kim and Jack use fake IDs to buy two liters of vodka, soda, limes, and snacks. Nina watches with a mix of concern and thrill. She's never bought alcohol illegally — the novelty appeals to her. She decides to stay, despite her instincts telling her this could go wrong.
**Narrative function:** Shows their teenage rebellion. For Kim and Jack, it's survival (alcohol as coping mechanism). For Nina, it's adventure.

## BEAT 6.4 — The Classroom
**Characters:** Kim, Jack, Nina
**Location:** Abandoned police station parking lot
**What happens:** They settle in the parking lot of an old, abandoned police station — their unofficial drinking spot. Cracked asphalt, broken streetlights, the ghost of authority. Kim finally asks: what happened in the LDI arena after he passed out? Jack and Nina begin explaining.
**Narrative function:** The location is chosen for thematic resonance — they're hiding in the ruins of law enforcement.

## BEAT 6.5 — LDI 101
**Characters:** Jack, Nina, Kim
**Location:** Abandoned police station
**What happens:** Jack and Nina deliver the exposition: LDI was created by Yohualticit in 2022. The ranking system (SDR). Monetization systems — SDA (betting), SDD (donations), SAL (limited access), STE (exclusive training), SDP (partnerships), SVN (NFTs). Kim learns that in one fight, he reached 1,201st globally. Jack is 10,776th. Nina is 8,724th. They're all "medium/high relevance world tops."
**Narrative function:** World-building delivery. The info is organic — Kim is genuinely curious, and Jack+Nina have differing expertise levels that let them alternate explanation duties.

## BEAT 6.6 — The Anonymity Question
**Characters:** Kim, Jack
**Location:** Abandoned police station
**What happens:** Kim's first concern: can he hide his identity? Jack confirms he can change his nickname and avatar to remain anonymous. Kim relaxes slightly. But Jack didn't warn him about the video already circulating on PowWow. Kim gets angry — he promised Kim anonymity. The tension passes when Jack apologizes, but the seed of distrust is planted.
**Narrative function:** Establishes Kim's need for control over his identity. Jack's oversight becomes a recurring issue.

## BEAT 6.7 — Drunken Confessions
**Characters:** Kim, Jack, Nina
**Location:** Abandoned police station
**What happens:** As the vodka flows, barriers drop. They talk about music — Jack plays drums, Nina plays bass. They talk about dreams, fears. The flirtation intensifies. Nina, emboldened by alcohol, says she'd "take both of them" but they "aren't man enough." It's a challenge, a flirt, and a test. Kim doesn't know how to respond.
**Narrative function:** The romantic triangle's first explicit tension. Nina's comment is deliberately provocative — she wants to see how they react. Jack laughs it off. Kim gets quiet.

## BEAT 6.8 — Walking Home
**Characters:** Kim, Jack, Nina
**Location:** Streets, then splitting paths
**What happens:** The night ends. They walk home together until their paths diverge. Promises to meet tomorrow. The trio is solid now — not just a school project group, but real friends. Kim arrives home buzzed, feeling lighter than he has in years. Helena is in a rare good mood.
**Narrative function:** Chapter closer. Establishes the new normal. Kim's life has gained something he didn't know he was missing: friends who fight beside him.
