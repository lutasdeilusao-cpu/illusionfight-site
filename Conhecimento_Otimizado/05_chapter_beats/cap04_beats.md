# CHAPTER 4: "O Pajé mais Cringe que existe?"

**Summary:** On the verge of death, Kim is transported to a spirit world where Pajé Yawanari — an ancient guardian who speaks like a modern teenager — offers him the Sangue Primordial. Kim accepts, transforms into Kim Primordial, and brutally annihilates Thunderbolt. The event triggers worldwide alarms at Yohualticit and attracts the attention of David Kronos.

**Key events:**
- Kim meets Pajé Yawanari in a spirit realm
- Yawanari speaks in modern slang, reveals he knows Kim's father
- Kim accepts the Sangue Primordial power
- Returns to the arena as Kim Primordial (white hair, tribal tattoos, black flames)
- Brutally dominates and devours Thunderbolt with black flames
- The arena is destroyed by the power surge
- Yohualticit alarms go off worldwide
- David Kronos celebrates the manifestation
- Kim jumps to rank 1201 globally
- Nina reveals she followed them, joins the group

**Characters present:** Kim, Pajé Yawanari, Thunderbolt, Jack, Nina, NexusPhantasm, David Kronos (remote)

**Tone:** Surreal, dark, cathartic, then ominous. The transformation is visually spectacular and deeply unsettling.

---

## BEAT 4.1 — The Spirit Realm
**Characters:** Kim, Pajé Yawanari
**Location:** Spirit world / Xakaxi ancestral plane
**What happens:** Kim opens his eyes to a misty otherworld. Ancient trees, floating embers, the smell of smoke and earth. A figure sits cross-legged before him — Pajé Yawanari, decorated in tribal paint and feathers. He opens his mouth and says: "E aí, véi. Tava na hora de você aparecer. Tava quase perdendo a paciência." Kim's confusion is immediate and total.
**Narrative function:** Yawanari's anachronistic speech immediately establishes his uniqueness. He is ancient but broken in a very modern way.

## BEAT 4.2 — The Offer
**Characters:** Kim, Pajé Yawanari
**Location:** Spirit world
**What happens:** Yawanari explains: he's a Xakaxi guardian, sealed the Sangue Primordial centuries ago to prevent catastrophe. The power is meant for Kim — has always been meant for him. He speaks of Kim's father with familiarity. "Você quer viver ou quer vencer?" Kim, thinking of the money, thinking of survival, says yes. Yawanari places a hand on his chest. Fire, water, wind, earth, and light flood through Kim's body.
**Narrative function:** The power is framed as inheritance, not charity. Yawanari's cryptic mention of Kim's father plants a major plot thread.

## BEAT 4.3 — The Transformation
**Characters:** Kim (becoming Kim Primordial)
**Location:** Spirit world → Arena
**What happens:** Kim's body convulses as the elements course through him. His black hair bleaches white, lengthening. Tribal tattoos bloom across his skin, ancient patterns of the Xakaxi. Black flames curl around his arms and torso. His eyes ignite. The spirit world shatters, and he crashes back into the arena — completely transformed. The crowd goes silent.
**Narrative function:** The visual transformation is the chapter's iconic moment. The black flames distinguish Kim from ordinary fire-element users.

## BEAT 4.4 — The Reckoning
**Characters:** Kim Primordial, Thunderbolt
**Location:** Arena
**What happens:** Thunderbolt turns to face his opponent and sees a demon. Kim moves faster than anything Thunderbolt has seen. He catches Thunderbolt's lightning in his bare hand, absorbs it. Then he begins. He doesn't just defeat Thunderbolt — he savors it. He drinks his blood, devours him with black flames that burn beyond the game. Thunderbolt screams in real-world agony.
**Narrative function:** Catharsis for the audience after Chapter 3's brutality. But also a warning: Kim's power is savage and barely controlled.

## BEAT 4.5 — The Shockwave
**Characters:** Kim Primordial, NexusPhantasm
**Location:** Arena
**What happens:** Kim's final attack unleashes a wave of black fire that cracks the arena foundation. The structure groans and partially collapses. NexusPhantasm's voice, usually theatrical, now carries genuine alarm: "The arena... it's destabilizing. This is not possible." Kim stands in the wreckage, white-haired, black-flamed, alone.
**Narrative function:** Shows the power exceeds the game's parameters. This is a universe-level anomaly, not just a strong player.

## BEAT 4.6 — Yohualticit Alarms
**Characters:** Yohualticit technicians (offscreen), David Kronos
**Location:** Yohualticit headquarters (offscreen)
**What happens:** Across Yohualticit's global facilities, alarms blare. Monitors flash red: "SANGUE PRIMORDIAL DETECTED." Technicians scramble. David Kronos, in his host body (an indigenous lawyer), watches the data stream and smiles. "Finally. After all these centuries... there you are." He rises, centuries of patient malice stirring.
**Narrative function:** Expands the scope. Kim's actions have global consequences. Introduces the main antagonist as a remote observer, building dread.

## BEAT 4.7 — The Aftermath
**Characters:** Kim (reverted), Jack, Nina
**Location:** Real world — Jack's setup
**What happens:** Kim disconnects from LDI, gasping. The power is gone. White hair fades to black. Tattoos vanish. He's just Kim again. Jack rushes over, half-celebrating, half-terrified. Kim checks his ranking: 1201. One fight. Jack shows him the video on PowWow — it's already going viral. Then Nina steps out of the shadows: "I followed you. I saw everything."
**Narrative function:** The trio is formed. Nina's decision to follow them shows her commitment. Kim's sudden fame has consequences he hasn't considered.

## BEAT 4.8 — The New Reality
**Characters:** Kim, Jack, Nina
**Location:** Near Jack's setup
**What happens:** Nina explains she's been watching since the annex fight. She saw the LDI match through Jack's stream. She's in. Kim is uncomfortable — another person in his business. Jack mediates. The three stand in awkward silence, processing. Kim is a phenomenon now. The world has changed.
**Narrative function:** Status quo shift. The duo is now a trio. Kim is no longer anonymous. The adventure has truly begun.
