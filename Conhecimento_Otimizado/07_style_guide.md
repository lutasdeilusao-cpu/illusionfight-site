# ISA UNIVERSE — WRITING STYLE GUIDE

## Voice & Tone

Street-level grit mixed with supernatural awe. Prose oscillates between visceral brutality in fight scenes and tender vulnerability in character moments. The narrator (NexusPhantasm in LDI) is theatrical and sardonic. Third-person limited, primarily from Kim's POV. Dark humor is a constant—even tragedy gets a cynical punchline.

## Prose Style

Short, punchy sentences for action. Lyrical, sensory-rich prose for emotional beats. Frequent use of onomatopoeia and italics for emphasis. Internal monologue marked by italics or (pensando) tags. Present tense for immediacy. Dialogue drives characterization more than description.

## Dialogue Rules

- **Kim speaks:** Sarcastic, defensive, short bursts. Uses street slang and dry wit. Leans into provocation when cornered. Calls Helena "vossa excelência" as mockery. Rarely opens up—when he does, it's clipped and raw.
- **Jack speaks:** Fast-talking, humorous, always scheming. Exaggerated confidence masking loyalty. Uses nicknames, rhetorical questions, and theatrical declarations ("hoje é dia do troco!"). Code-switches between smooth operator and genuine brother.
- **Nina speaks:** Direct, cutting, initially arrogant. Short, precise sentences. Drops pleasantries entirely. Later reveals warmth through teasing and dry humor. Calls people out without hesitation.
- **Helena speaks:** Abrasive, defensive, manipulative in early chapters. Heavy with profanity. Shifts to fragile and vulnerable after her reveal. Uses "pivete" and "merda" as default address.
- **Pajé Yawanari speaks:** Wildly anachronistic. Ancient spiritual wisdom delivered in modern slang ("véi", "foda", "cringe"). Contrast between mystic appearance and internet-addicted speech. Calls himself "Vovô Descolado."

## Action Scenes

Fights are brutal, technical, and physically descriptive. Every blow has consequence. Joints break, blood sprays, bones snap. The line between virtual and real pain is blurred—LDI injuries feel 100% authentic. Fights use real martial arts terminology (hooks, uppercuts, Muay Thai clinch, jiu-jitsu positional awareness). In LDI, elemental powers and weapon manifestations (Xakixi, mode Xamã) add a supernatural layer. Kim fights like a brawler turned predator; Jack fights with showmanship; Nina fights with technical precision.

## What to Avoid

- Over-explaining systems mid-action (save mechanics for training chapters)
- Sentimental melodrama without cynical counterbalance
- Making the villains one-dimensionally evil without moments of charisma
- Heavy exposition dumps in dialogue ("As you know...")
- Romanticizing poverty or Helena's abuse—show the ugliness without gloss
- Unrealistic forgiveness arcs—characters earn their changes

## Cultural Notes

- Bravara is Brazil-coded: social dynamics, colonial history, street culture, music, family structures. DIX is the local instant payment system (analogous to Pix).
- Indigenous identity is central to Kim. Xakaxi roots are not decorative—they carry history, trauma, and power (Sangue Primordial). Avoid stereotypical "noble savage" framing; the Xakaxi were technologically advanced, not primitive.
- Violence is a survival tool, not glamorized. Every fight costs Kim something.
- Wealth inequality is a constant background pressure. Elite Academy exists as a stark class contrast.
- Age-appropriate content: characters are 17, written with authenticity about teenage life (drinking, fighting, poverty, crushes) without being gratuitous.
